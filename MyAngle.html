My Angle

Hello, my name is Lauren Gottlieb, I'm 19 years old, my birthday is October 2d, 2002, and my pronouns are she/her. I am currently enrolled in the Faculty of Liberal Art User Experience Design program and am in my second year. I work as a horseback riding instructor and as a horse trainer; this takes up most of my time aside from school because I also take horse riding lessons myself. I enjoy reading fiction books, binge-watching shows about real estate I could probably never afford and am currently awaiting the start of my volleyball season. An interesting fact about me is an I used to own a small business with a group of other high school students but liquidated when COVID hit. I was the Vice President of Information Technology.

I have always seen myself as a leader. I wanted to take charge from an early age and be an excellent example for people to follow. I felt this course would help me develop my skills as a leader and the mindset needed; I wanted to learn how to play to my strengths and surpass my weaknesses. 

Intelligence, Confidence, Charisma, Determination, Sociability, and Integrity are the six critical leadership traits. Out of these six traits, I feel the strongest are confidence, charisma and sociability. I am still intelligent, determined and display integrity, but these traits require more effort and don't come as naturally.    

There are many different types of followers; I display the qualities of a effective  follower. I can think for myself and carry out my tasks with readiness, energy and determination. I am very assertive because I believe in taking risks and being an independent problem solver; I work well with coworkers and superiors, always doing my best to remain helpful to others and positive. The follower type I am least like is the sheep; I pride myself on going above and beyond and always giving my best to my team. 

When I received my results, I was surprised by how close the results were between Authoritative and Democratic. Honestly, I thought authoritative would be higher. After all, I always accept people best because I want them to be the best workers. The lack of laissez-faire made perfect sense, I'm not one to let things slide and let people do whatever they want and govern themselves; I like to be checked in. Moving forward, I would like to work on being less demanding and less strict. The type of organization I see myself in is either freelance, consultation work, or entrepreneurship.
